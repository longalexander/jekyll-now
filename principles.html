Principles


Care for others

When thoughts are racing through my head, I find it difficult to concentrate on the person I am interacting with.

Once my thoughts have calmed down, I inevitably relive recent occasions where I've failed to concentrate on the person I'm interacting with. I feel disappointing, grimacing, regret.

I feel regret because I have been in the shoes of the other person. I have interacted with people who were not focused on me and those people made me feel lonely and insignificant. Now, I am the one making someone else feel lonely and insignificant. I think that this is wrong.

Stick With It

When I've decided that something is worth doing, I should stick with it to completion (unless my initial assumptions were wrong).

Nothing worthwhile can be achieved on a short time scale.

Choose your heroes carefully

Things often go wrong because we place undue emphasize on things that make us miss the things that will actually lead to positive outcomes.

Choosing heroes unwisely can lead us to place undue emphasis on goals (e.g. earning lots of money) that can lead to negative outcomes over time (e.g. fraud).

A more nuanced example would be over-emphasizing the importance of grit and overcoming obstacles at the expense of recognizing that sheer enjoyment of a task/problem can often spur someone to unconsciously act in ways which we consider 'gritty' and is often much more sustainable in the long run.

Help people when they're down

People will always be nice when you're doing well.

Look out for people who are well-meaning but going through a dip.

Listen to your body

Biology imposes limits. Violating them often results in less than desirable outcomes. Your body has its own strengths and weaknesses - stack the deck in your favour.
