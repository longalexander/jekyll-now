I also plan to use this site as a repository for a guidebook I'm writing that will help people understand REIT financial statements. Consider this: Public companies report their financial results to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). These filings are valuable for company insiders and outsiders. For outsiders, they are the closest things to facts about the companies. For insiders, SEC filings can be a tool for understanding the history of the company and gaining insight into management's decisions. Accounting (the language used to describe businesses) includes new terminology and non-intuitive concepts which means that there is some initial friction to understanding financial statements. For some businesses, friction is increased because Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (i.e. the rules of accounting) don't accurately reflect the state of that business. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) are one of these businesses. To help smooth out some of the bumps in the road, I'm writing a guidebook to help beginners understand REIT financial statements. I am using Prologis (NYSE:PLD) as an illustrative case study.
