For the purposes of this page, "principles" are defined to be: rules that help me make decisions by illuminating a clearer distinction between what I believe to be right and wrong.

I am occasionally lucky enough to discover principles which make my life easier. You may attempt to use them in your own life, but I must warn you that they may not work because they were designed to suit my needs at a particular time and place.


Choosing Classes

I attend a university where I have a lot of freedom in the classes I choose to take.

Personally, I try to maximize my ability to learn after college. For me, this means strengthening thinking skills which I believe I will need in the future. University is a perfect place for me to strengthen these skills because I believe that it is important to build good habits while the cost of failure is low and university provides a lot of low cost, high quality resources to support the development of reasoning skills.

Principle: If you discover that you are weak at a skill which interests you, empowers you and which makes you envy others who possess it, work to develop it.
